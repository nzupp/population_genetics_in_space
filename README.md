# Population Genetics in Space

Long duration space missions to Mars will require bioregenerative life support systems (BLSS) to sustain crew members. These systems rely on crop populations maintained across multiple generations to provide oxygen regeneration, CO2 scrubbing, waste recycling, and fresh food. Continuous breeding populations are mandatory for 2-5 year missions, making their genetic health under spaceflight conditions a critical and unsolved problem. Space exposes plants to high radiation that accelerates mutation accumulation, while the physical constraints of missions confine them to small populations prone to inbreeding depression. Mutation load reduces plant fitness and inbreeding depression reduces vigor and yield, yet no framework exists to quantify or manage these pressures in mission contexts. Here I use forward-time population genetic simulations to quantify how radiation-elevated mutation rates and small population sizes impact mutational load and inbreeding coefficients under Mars mission conditions across crops with 30-120 day generation times (lettuce, tomato, wheat).
